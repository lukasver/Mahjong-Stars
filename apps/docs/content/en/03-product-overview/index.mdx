# Gameplay Mechanics

Mahjong Stars' product offering is designed to deliver a comprehensive and engaging gameplay experience while providing a robust framework for future expansion.

## Supported Mahjong Variants

Mahjong Stars recognises that Mahjong has many regional variants, each with unique rules and scoring systems. The platform aims to support the most popular versions and provide customisation options for house rules.

### Launch Variants

#### Riichi (Japanese Mahjong)

Known for its strategic depth, Riichi features complex scoring patterns, concealed hands and the riichi declaration. It appeals to competitive players who enjoy intricate tactical play.

**Key Features**:
- Official tournament rules implementation
- Optional house rule variations
- Dora and uradora bonus tiles
- Furiten rules and restrictions
- Complex yaku (scoring patterns) system
- Strategic depth and probability calculation

**Target Audience**: Competitive players, strategy enthusiasts, tournament participants

<Callout type="success">
Mahjong Stars implements Riichi with full tournament-standard rules, making it ideal for serious competitive play.
</Callout>

#### Bang Bang (Casual Mahjong)

This variant simplifies certain aspects of Mahjong to make the game more accessible for beginners. Bang Bang emphasises fast play, straightforward scoring and a shorter learning curve.

**Key Features**:
- Simplified scoring system
- Faster game pace
- Beginner-friendly rules
- Social-oriented gameplay
- Reduced complexity
- Quick rounds (15-20 minutes)

**Target Audience**: Casual players, beginners, social gamers

### Future Variants

Future updates will introduce additional rule sets:

#### Cantonese/Hong Kong Mahjong

A popular variant in Southern China and Hong Kong with flower tiles and a fan-based scoring system.

**Planned Features**:
- Flower and season tiles
- Fan-based scoring
- Minimum point requirements
- Fast-paced gameplay
- Social betting options (non-monetary)

**Target Launch**: Phase 2 (2026 Q3-2027 Q1)

#### American Mahjong

Featuring Jokers and special combinations, American Mahjong differs significantly from Asian rule sets. It is widely played in the United States and has a dedicated following.

**Planned Features**:
- Joker tiles
- Annual card changes
- Charleston tile passing
- Unique hand patterns
- Different tile set (optional)

**Target Launch**: Phase 2 (2026 Q3-2027 Q1)

#### Singaporean and Korean Variants

These versions offer unique tile sets and play dynamics. Adding them will broaden Mahjong Stars' appeal in different regions.

**Target Launch**: Phase 2-3 (2027)

<Callout type="info">
**Customization**: Beyond standard variants, players can host matches using custom house rules, attracting friends and communities from around the world.
</Callout>

## Tournaments, Leaderboards and Ranked Matches

Tournaments and ranking systems are essential for sustaining a competitive community.

### Global Leaderboards

A real-time leaderboard displays top players across different categories.

**Leaderboard Categories**:
- **Casual Rating**: Relaxed play performance
- **Ranked Rating**: Competitive skill level
- **Clan Ranking**: Team performance
- **AI Performance**: AI companion effectiveness
- **Tournament Points**: Competition results
- **Variant-Specific**: Rankings per Mahjong variant

**Visibility Benefits**:
- Recognition for skilled players
- Motivation for improvement
- Community status
- Tournament seeding
- Sponsorship opportunities

### Seasonal Rankings

Rankings are divided into seasons, with resets and rewards at the end of each season.

**Season Structure**:
- **Duration**: 3 months per season
- **Resets**: Regular refresh prevents stagnation
- **Rewards**: Exclusive cosmetics, characters, studio slots
- **Placement Matches**: New season calibration
- **Decay System**: Inactivity penalties

**Benefits**:
- Prevents dominant player monopoly
- Continuous motivation
- Fresh starts for new players
- Regular reward distribution
- Seasonal themes and events

### Multi-tier Tournaments

Different tournament scales for different commitment levels.

**Daily Tournaments**:
- Entry fee: 100-500 soft currency
- Duration: 2-4 hours
- Prize pool: 10,000-50,000 soft currency
- Participants: 32-128 players

**Weekly Tournaments**:
- Entry fee: 500-2,000 soft currency
- Duration: Full day or weekend
- Prize pool: 100,000-500,000 soft currency + items
- Participants: 128-512 players

**Seasonal Championships**:
- Entry: Qualification required
- Duration: Multi-week with finals
- Prize pool: Substantial (rare characters, tokens, recognition)
- Participants: Top-ranked players
- Live broadcasts: Professional production

### Clan Competitions

Clans can compete in clan-only tournaments, where teams of players accumulate points based on individual performances.

**Clan Tournament Features**:
- **Team Points**: Collective scoring system
- **Clan vs Clan**: Direct team matchups
- **Rank Requirements**: Minimum clan size and activity
- **Prizes**: Clan-wide rewards and bonuses
- **Prestige**: Clan reputation and rankings

**Benefits**:
- Teamwork and strategy development
- Cross-player collaboration
- Community building
- Shared goals and achievements
- Clan identity

## Addressing Current Online Mahjong Issues

Existing online Mahjong platforms face several challenges that Mahjong Stars directly addresses:

### Problem 1: Lack of Fairness

**Industry Issue**: Some platforms have been accused of bias in tile distributions or matching algorithms, undermining trust.

**Mahjong Stars Solution**:
- Provably fair randomization
- Player-trained AI (no rigged bots)
- Transparent algorithms
- Regular audits
- Public algorithm documentation

### Problem 2: Limited Regional Support

**Industry Issue**: Many games restrict players to regional rule sets, limiting global interaction.

**Mahjong Stars Solution**:
- Multiple variant support
- Cross-regional play
- Customizable rule sets
- Cultural inclusivity
- Global player matching

### Problem 3: Absence of High-Level Competition

**Industry Issue**: Few platforms offer robust tournament systems or meaningful rankings.

**Mahjong Stars Solution**:
- Structured competitive modes
- Professional tournament systems
- Clear ranking progression
- Attractive prize pools
- Esports potential

### Problem 4: Poor Monetisation Models

**Industry Issue**: Pay-to-win mechanics or excessive advertising drive away serious players.

**Mahjong Stars Solution**:
- Soft currency earnable through gameplay
- No pay-to-win mechanics
- Skill-based success
- Optional cosmetic purchases
- Fair entry fee tournaments
- Sustainable economy

<Callout type="success">
**Fair Play Guarantee**: By enabling each player to have their own AI trained on their own behavior and skill level, there are no platform-controlled bots that could be rigged.
</Callout>
