## 1.2 The Game of Mahjong: Cultural Significance and Global Appeal

Mahjong is a tile-based game that originated in China and has been played for over a
century. Its rules involve strategic tile-matching, pattern recognition and a balance of
offensive and defensive tactics. With variations such as Riichi (Japanese), Cantonese

(Hong Kong), American and Singaporean Mahjong, the game has adapted to diverse
cultural contexts while retaining its fundamental appeal.

Cultural Significance: The game is deeply embedded in the social fabric of East Asian
cultures, often associated with family gatherings, celebrations and intellectual
competition. Its ability to bring people together across generations has turned Mahjong
into a cultural icon.

Global Appeal: Today, Mahjong is played by hundreds of millions worldwide. Experts
estimate that at least 600 million people enjoy the game. The spread of Mahjong to
Western countries, along with the rise of digital platforms, has fueled its continued
popularity. Mahjong's combination of skill, chance and social interaction appeals to a
broad audience, making it ripe for innovation.

## 3.1 Gameplay Mechanics

### 3.1.1 Supported Mahjong Variants

Mahjong Stars recognises that Mahjong has many regional variants, each with unique
rules and scoring systems. The platform aims to support the most popular versions and
provide customisation options for house rules. The initial launch focuses on:

- Riichi (Japanese Mahjong): Known for its strategic depth, Riichi features complex
  scoring patterns, concealed hands and the riichi declaration. It appeals to
  competitive players who enjoy intricate tactical play. Mahjong Stars implements
  Riichi with official tournament rules and optional house rule variations.
- Bang Bang (Casual Mahjong): This variant simplifies certain aspects of Mahjong to
  make the game more accessible for beginners. Bang Bang emphasises fast play,
  straightforward scoring and a shorter learning curve, making it ideal for casual and
  social matches.

Future updates will introduce additional rule sets, including but not limited to:

- Cantonese/Hong Kong Mahjong: A popular variant in Southern China and Hong
  Kong with flower tiles and a fan-based scoring system.
- American Mahjong: Featuring Jokers and special combinations, American Mahjong
  differs significantly from Asian rule sets. It is widely played in the United States and
  has a dedicated following.
- Singaporean and Korean Variants: These versions offer unique tile sets and play
  dynamics. Adding them will broaden Mahjong Stars' appeal in different regions.

By offering multiple rule sets, the platform ensures inclusivity and fosters cross-regional
play. Players can host matches using their preferred rules, attracting friends and
communities from around the world.

### 3.1.2 Casual and Competitive Play Modes

Mahjong Stars provides a variety of play modes to cater to different player preferences:

- Casual Matches: For those who want a relaxed experience, casual matches can be
  set up quickly with friends or strangers. Players can choose table settings, including
  the number of rounds, time limits and stake levels. Casual play emphasises fun and
  social interaction over ranking and rewards.
- Ranked Games: Competitive players can participate in ranked games that
  contribute to their global ranking. The ranking system considers win rates, skill level
  and match difficulty. Rankings reset periodically to ensure fairness and give new
  players an opportunity to climb the leaderboards.

- Tournaments: Mahjong Stars hosts daily, weekly and seasonal tournaments with
  various formats, such as single-elimination, Swiss or round-robin. Tournaments offer
  larger prize pools, including rare characters, charm bundles or soft currency. Some
  tournaments may require entry fees (paid in soft currency or tokens) and feature
  multi-round events culminating in live-streamed finals.
- Practice and Training Mode: Beginners can practice against Al opponents at
  varying difficulty levels. Training mode includes optional hints and allows players to
  replay and analyse hands with built-in tools.
- Private Rooms: Players can create password-protected tables to play with friends,
  apply house rules or host club events. Private rooms support voice chat and video
  integration, replicating the feel of a real-world Mahjong parlour.

### 3.1.3 Tournaments, Leaderboards and Ranked Matches

Tournaments and ranking systems are essential for sustaining a competitive community.
Mahjong Stars implements:

- Global Leaderboards: A real-time leaderboard displays top players across different
  categories, including casual rating, ranked rating, clan ranking and Al performance
  ranking. Leaderboards increase visibility for skilled players and encourage others to
  improve.
- Seasonal Rankings: Rankings are divided into seasons, with resets and rewards at
  the end of each season. Seasonal resets prevent dominant players from holding the
  top spots indefinitely and motivate continuous play. Rewards may include exclusive
  cosmetics, characters or studio slots.
- Multi-tier Tournaments: Daily tournaments offer quick competitions with small entry
  fees and modest prizes. Weekly tournaments provide larger prize pools and longer
  formats. Seasonal championships serve as flagship events, featuring qualifiers, live
  broadcasts and substantial rewards.
- Clan Competitions: Clans can compete in clan-only tournaments, where teams of
  players accumulate points based on individual performances. Clan competitions
  foster teamwork and cross-player strategy development.

## 3.2 Hybrid Al Integration

The integration of artificial intelligence sets Mahjong Stars apart. Rather than relying on
static bots, the platform uses a dynamic Al system that evolves with player input and
generates personalised experiences. Al in Mahjong Stars operates at several levels:

### 3.2.1 Al-Assisted Gameplay and Strategy Hints

During matches, the Al monitors the game state, player decisions and available tiles. It
then provides:

- Real-Time Hints: Players can toggle hints to receive subtle suggestions on tile
  discards, meld opportunities or risk assessments. Hints are context-sensitive and
  adjust to the player's level, ensuring that novices receive more guidance while
  experienced players are given high-level insights.
- Post-Game Analysis: After each match, the Al generates a report highlighting key
  decisions, strategic mistakes and alternative lines of play. Players can review
  replays, examine the Al's recommendations and share analyses with friends or
  coaches.
- Adaptive Learning: The Al learns from the player's responses to hints. If a player
  consistently ignores certain advice, the Al adjusts its recommendations. Over time,
  hints become finely tuned to each individual's learning style.

### 3.2.2 Personalized Al Players and Offline Competition

Beyond coaching, Mahjong Stars introduces Al companions that simulate the player's
own style. This feature operates as follows:

- Data Collection: When a player participates in matches, the Al collects data on
  decisions, timing, pattern recognition, risk tolerance and tile combinations. The data
  is anonymised and stored securely.
- Behaviour Modeling: The Al uses machine learning to identify patterns in the
  player's behaviour, constructing a behavioural model. This model captures high-level
  decision heuristics as well as subtle tendencies, such as preference for certain
  melds or risk profiles.
- Al Companion Creation: Once sufficient data is gathered, the player can instantiate
  an Al companion. This Al is linked to a chosen character asset and can participate in
  matches autonomously. The Al's performance evolves as more data is collected and
  as the player trains it through feedback or additional matches.
- Offline Play: Players can deploy their Al companions to compete in matches or
  tournaments when they are offline. The Al will earn soft currency, rewards or ranking

points on behalf of the user. Players can set risk thresholds, specify play modes
(casual, ranked or tournament) and monitor Al performance remotely.

- Al Rental and Team Play: Advanced players may choose to rent their trained Al
  companions to others. Rental fees can be paid in soft currency or tokens, with the
  platform taking a small commission. Teams of Al companions can also participate in
  special events where coordinated Al behaviour is required.

### 3.2.3 Fairness and Game Dynamics Enhancement

Al is also deployed to maintain fairness and balance across the platform. The system
monitors player behaviour, tile distributions and match outcomes to detect anomalies
such as:

- Collusion or Teaming: If players repeatedly form partnerships to target others, the
  Al flags the behaviour and intervenes. Penalties may include temporary suspensions
  or reduced rewards.
- Cheating or Bots: External cheating software or bots that attempt to exploit the
  game are identified through pattern analysis. Accounts using such tools are
  investigated and sanctioned.
- Strategy Dominance: The Al analyses meta trends and win rates to ensure that no
  single strategy becomes overwhelmingly dominant. If a particular tile combination or
  playstyle appears unbalanced, developers can adjust game parameters to
  reintroduce diversity.

This dynamic monitoring maintains a healthy competitive environment and reduces
opportunities for exploitation.

### 3.2.4 Innovative Gameplay Features

To further elevate the Mahjong experience, Mahjong Stars introduces innovations such
as:

- 24/7 Liquidity: With Al companions able to play on behalf of users, there are always
  active matches available. Players no longer wait for opponents; they can join tables
  instantly at any time.
- Hybrid Play Mode: Players can switch seamlessly between manual and Al-driven
  play. For example, a player might start a match manually, then hand control over to
  their Al companion mid-game if they need to step away.
- Clan Al Training: Clans can pool data to train clan-specific Al companions that
  embody collective strategies. These clan Als can compete in inter-clan tournaments,
  adding a layer of cooperative strategy and team identity.
- Al Customisation: Players can adjust their Al's aggression level, defensive
  tendencies and risk profile using simple sliders. Advanced users may access deeper
  settings or even design custom heuristics for their Al companions.

These innovations provide depth and variety, ensuring that Mahjong Stars remains
engaging for years to come.

## 3.3 Collectibles and Digital Assets

True ownership of in-game assets is also made possible by Mahjong Stars. By offering
the possibility to leverage blockchain technology, the platform allows players to possess,
trade and monetize their in-game items if the player wishes to. If the player does not,
then they may keep them as traditional in-game assets that still provide boosts or other
advantages within the gameplay. Collectibles fall into three main categories: characters,
charms and studios.

### 3.3.1 Character Assets: Acquisition, Leveling and Al Enablement

Characters serve as the primary avatars through which players engage with the game.
Each character asset has unique attributes, aesthetics and story elements that give it
personality. Character assets function as follows:

- Acquisition: Players obtain characters via soft currency purchases, token-backed
  sales, tournament rewards or special events. Some characters are common and
  easy to acquire, while others are rare or limited edition.
- Attributes and Skill Trees: Characters come with base attributes such as luck,
  strategy or social charisma. As players use their characters, they gain experience
  points, level up and unlock additional abilities or cosmetics. Skill trees might include
  sections for improving Al compatibility, boosting soft currency earnings or unlocking
  unique chat emojis.
- Al Slots: Each character has a certain number of Al slots, determining how many
  personalised Al companions can be attached. High-rarity characters may support
  multiple Al companions, enabling advanced strategies and team play.
- Evolution and Fusion: Players can merge duplicate characters to evolve them into
  higher-rarity versions. Evolution may change the character's appearance, unlock
  new voice lines or increase Al capacity.
- Narrative Lore: Characters are embedded within a narrative universe. Collecting
  different characters unlocks story chapters, deepening player engagement.

By combining gameplay utility with narrative appeal, character assets become desirable
collectibles that hold value both inside and outside the game.

### 3.3.2 Charm Assets: Rewards, Raffles and Passive Earning

Charms are supplementary items that grant bonuses or aesthetic flair. They are
designed to encourage consistent gameplay and provide players with long-term goals.

- Types of Charms: Charms come in various formsâ€”some are purely cosmetic, such
  as table decorations or special tile designs; others offer gameplay benefits, like
  increased soft currency drop rates or enhanced character experience gain. Rare
  charms might grant access to exclusive tournaments or content.
- Acquisition: Charms are typically awarded through daily quests, achievements,
  raffles, or in special events. They may also drop randomly from winning matches or
  be purchasable with soft currency.
- Raffles and Loot Boxes: Mahjong Stars uses raffles to distribute rare charms.
  Players obtain raffle tickets through gameplay or purchase them with soft currency.
  Draws are provably random, and winning a raffle can yield valuable charms or even
  characters.
- Passive Earning: Charms can transform into the blockchain version in order to be
  staked in a rewards pool to earn additional soft currency or token rewards. The

staking mechanism encourages players to hold onto charms and participate in the
economic ecosystem.

- Charm Evolution: Similar to characters, charms can be combined or upgraded. For
  example, combining three common charms might yield one rare charm with
  enhanced benefits.

Charms add a collectible layer beyond characters, catering to players who enjoy
incremental progression and surprise rewards.

### 3.3.3 Studio Assets: Managing Multiple Characters and Al Assignment

Studios represent virtual spaces where players manage teams of characters and Al
companions. They are the pinnacle of collectible assets within Mahjong Stars and are
designed for players who want to orchestrate complex strategies.

- Acquisition: Studios are limited in number and are typically sold via auctions or
  high-tier tournament rewards. Owning a studio grants the right to host multiple
  characters and Al companions.
- Team Management: Within a studio, players organise characters into squads. Each
  squad can be assigned an Al companion, enabling simultaneous participation in
  multiple matches or tournaments. Studio owners can rotate characters and Al
  companions depending on the event.
- Al Training Facilities: Studios house Al training facilities where players can
  accelerate the learning process by providing targeted data sets or running simulated
  matches. Advanced analytics help fine-tune Al behaviours.
- Renting and Sponsorship: Studio owners may rent out unused characters or Al
  companions to other players, earning passive income. Studios can also become
  sponsors, providing characters to up-and-coming players in exchange for a share of
  their winnings.
- Customisation and Prestige: Studios can be visually customised with decorations,
  trophies and clan insignia. High-level studios gain prestige bonuses, such as
  increased soft currency earnings or enhanced training efficiency.

Studios appeal to players who enjoy strategy at a macro level, offering an avenue for
greater investment and deeper involvement in the ecosystem.

Together, these social features transform Mahjong Stars from a solitary pastime into a

## 5 Game Economy and Tokens

The Mahjong Stars economy is designed to balance accessibility with sustainability. By
employing a dual currency system and carefully structured tokenomics, the platform
accommodates different types of players and investors.

### 5.1 Dual Currency System

#### 5.1.1 Soft Currency (In-Game Credits)

Soft currency is either purchased or earned primarily through gameplay and functions as
the day-to-day currency of Mahjong Stars. It is used for:

- Purchasing characters and charms from the primary store.
- Paying table and entry fees for casual tournaments or raffles.
- Accelerating Al training or purchasing Al modules.
- Customising studios with decorations or unlocking theme packs.

Soft currency is designed to be non-speculative. While players can buy soft currency
with fiat or convert tokens into it, the exchange rate is fixed to prevent inflation or
deflation. This ensures that new players are not priced out of the game and that
gameplay remains skill-based rather than pay-to-win.

#### 5.1.2 Hard Currency / Token (Governance and Marketplace)

Alongside soft currency, Mahjong Stars utilises a blockchain-based token that performs
several roles:

- Governance: Token holders can vote on proposals related to game features, Al
  model parameters, tournament rules, economic adjustments and community
  initiatives. Proposals may include adding new Mahjong variants, adjusting character
  drop rates or allocating development budgets. Governance ensures that players and
  investors have a voice in shaping the platform.
- Marketplace Transactions: While characters, charms and studios can be bought
  with soft currency, marketplace transactions for trading these assets are
  denominated in the governance token. This encourages liquidity and allows sellers
  to capture the full value of their assets.
- Staking: Holders can stake tokens to earn rewards, gain access to premium
  features or support network security (depending on the underlying blockchain).
- Al Upgrades: Advanced Al training modules and premium analytics tools may
  require payment in the governance token. This ties the token to core gameplay
  features.

Because the token is issued and managed by a separate company, Mahjong Stars can
operate the game independently of regulatory constraints related to token issuance.
Players who wish to enjoy Mahjong Stars without participating in the token economy are
free to do so.

- VIP Features: Certain premium features, such as priority access to new characters

## 9 Glossary of Terms

This glossary defines key terms used throughout the whitepaper to aid understanding for
readers new to Mahjong, blockchain or Al technologies.

- Al Companion: A personalised artificial intelligence agent that learns from a player's
  behaviour and can play matches on their behalf.
- Bang Bang: A simplified Mahjong variant aimed at casual players, emphasising
  speed and accessibility.
- Blockchain: A decentralised, distributed ledger technology used to record
  transactions across multiple computers in a way that is secure, transparent and
  immutable.
- Character Asset: A unique digital item representing a playable avatar within
  Mahjong Stars. Characters have attributes and support Al attachments.
- Charm Asset: A collectible item that grants bonuses, cosmetics or passive income
  within Mahjong Stars.
- Clan: A group of players who band together for social and competitive purposes.
  Clans can compete in tournaments and share resources.
- Governance Token: The blockchain-based currency used for voting on proposals,
  paying marketplace fees and accessing premium features within Mahjong Stars.
- Hybrid Al: A system that combines traditional programmed Al with machine learning
  models that learn from human behaviour. In Mahjong Stars, hybrid Al assists players
  and forms personalised Al companions.
- Meld: A combination of Mahjong tiles forming a valid set (e.g., Pung, Chow, Kong).
  Melds contribute to a winning hand.
- MStars: A non-transferable reward currency used within Mahjong Stars to redeem
  for soft currency, assets or event entries.
- NFT (Non-Fungible Token): A unique digital asset recorded on a blockchain that
  represents ownership of a specific item or piece of content. Characters, charms and
  studios are implemented as NFTs.
- Pung: A set of three identical Mahjong tiles.
- Riichi: A popular Japanese Mahjong variant that emphasises strategy and
  concealed hands, known for its riichi declaration mechanic.
- Soft Currency: Earnable in-game credits used to purchase standard assets, enter
  tournaments and perform routine upgrades in Mahjong Stars
- Studio: A virtual space where players manage multiple character assets and Al
  companions. Studios allow team play, Al training acceleration and monetisation
  through rentals.
- Tokenomics: The study and design of the economic model governing a blockchain
  token's supply, distribution and utility.
- Tournament: A structured competition in which players or teams compete for prizes.
  Tournaments can be daily, weekly, seasonal or special events.
- Xi Wind: One of the four wind tiles in Mahjong, representing one of the cardinal
  directions. Wind tiles can form part of melds or score bonuses.
